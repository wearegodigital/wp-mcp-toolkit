# Generator Media — Content Audit & Recommendations

**Prepared by:** Sean Wilkinson
**Date:** February 16, 2026
**Source material:** "Generator Credentials for Clients" PDF (approved tone/branding) vs. live site at generatormedia.com

---

## Executive Summary

The credentials PDF represents a significantly updated brand voice, positioning, and set of key messages that are **not yet reflected on the website**. The site is running on older messaging that is more generic and doesn't leverage the bold, differentiated positioning in the approved credentials. There are also entire sections of content from the PDF that have no home on the site at all.

The biggest gaps:
1. **Different tagline and positioning** — site uses old language, PDF has new
2. **Key differentiators are completely different** between PDF and site
3. **Major brand concepts missing from site** entirely (Client Growth Accelerator®, The Generator Effect, WOAS, etc.)
4. **Company stats and proof points** absent from the site
5. **Client testimonials with attribution** not on the site
6. **Tone mismatch** — site is safe/generic, PDF is bold and direct

## Additional Content I'd Recommend Requesting

These items aren't in the PDF but would strengthen the site based on what the credentials are trying to communicate:

1. **Updated case studies with results** — The client success page just shows brand names. The PDF references specific outcomes (e.g., "re-optimizing investment to accelerate growth" for Prime Storage). Need detailed write-ups with metrics.

2. **Synapse platform screenshots or demo video** — The analytics page describes tools but has no visuals. A product demo or dashboard screenshot would strengthen credibility.

3. **Team bios** — The company page lists names and titles but no bios. The PDF emphasizes senior involvement and expertise — written bios would support this.

4. **Fresh thought leadership content** — Blog has articles from 2023. Need new pieces aligned to the updated positioning (WOAS, brand as growth driver, Generator Effect).

5. **Client logo permissions** — Verify the logos on the site match the current client roster. PDF mentions "23 active clients" but lists no category conflicts "for Webflow" — suggesting this was pitched to a prospect. Current site client list may differ.

6. **Certification/partner logos** — "Certified By" section appears on multiple pages. Need to verify which certifications and ensure logos are current.

7. **"We Actually Give a Sh*t" treatment** — This is their most memorable differentiator. If brand guidelines allow it on the website, it should be prominently featured. If not suitable for web, need an approved alternative that carries the same energy.

8. **Media partner logos** — PDF references a media ecosystem slide with Search & Social, Streaming Video, Audio & Podcast, Display & Native, and DSP categories. Need individual partner logos if they want to show this on the site.
